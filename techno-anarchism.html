Could AI & Robotics Build a Truly Free Society?

Exploring how automation, robotics, commons governance, and voluntary cooperation could dissolve the historic left–right divide within anarchism — and open a path beyond coercion.

The Core Thesis

Humanity already possesses the technical capacity to feed, house, heal, and educate every person on Earth. The problem is not scarcity — it is who owns, governs, and benefits from the machines. Two traditions have long argued that the state is the wrong answer. One comes from the left (Kropotkin, Bookchin, Ostrom). The other from the right (Mises, Hayek, Rothbard). They agree on 80% of the diagnosis. AI and robotics dissolve most of the remaining 20%.

🤖

Full Automation Transforms Everything

When marginal production cost approaches zero, the historic disputes over property, wages, and markets lose their material basis. The question shifts from who gets what to how do we coordinate abundance.

🌐

Distributed AI ≠ Central Planning

Federated AI — autonomous agents coordinating through open protocols — is structurally analogous to Hayek's price system: it aggregates local knowledge without any central node possessing global information. It solves the Mises-Hayek calculation problem without recreating the state.

🤝

Commons Are Spontaneous Order

Elinor Ostrom (Nobel 2009) proved that communities self-govern shared resources without private property or state regulation. Her polycentric governance is structurally isomorphic with Hayek's catallaxy — voluntary, adaptive, emergent from below.

⚡

Scarcity Dissolves, So Does the Debate

Left anarchists and anarcho-capitalists disagree about how to manage scarcity. Under post-scarcity enabled by AI+robotics, private property stops being oppressive (zero marginal cost), markets become optional, and the state becomes unnecessary for redistribution.

🦾

Robotics: From Labour to Liberation

Humanoid robots and autonomous systems are entering agriculture, manufacturing, and logistics. When machines handle all instrumental labour, humans are freed for care, creativity, community, and science — the real work of civilisation.

🔗

Crypto-Economic Tools for the Commons

Smart contracts, DAOs, and community tokens — originally from the crypto-anarchist right — can be repurposed for transparent, egalitarian commons governance. Combined with Huerta de Soto's 100% reserve free banking, they enable capital formation without conventional banks or states.

The Bridge: Where Left & Right Anarchism Converge

The media portrays anarcho-capitalism and anarcho-socialism as polar opposites. In reality, they share the same enemy (the state), the same method (voluntary cooperation), and the same dream (a free society). Technology makes their convergence not just possible but inevitable.

Essential Reading

Frontier research at the intersection of AI, post-scarcity economics, and voluntary governance — from both sides of the anarchist spectrum.

Frontier 2025

I, for one, welcome our robot overlords

Michael Huemer · AI & Society

The libertarian philosopher argues that AI will bring massive benefits to humanity. The real risk is not autonomous machines but human misuse. A rare optimistic voice from analytic philosophy.

Post-Scarcity 2025

Artificially Created Scarcity: How AI Turns Abundance into Shortage

Milton Ayoki · MPRA

Technology produces abundance, but institutional design creates artificial scarcity. The exact diagnosis that motivates commons-based alternatives.

Economics 2025

The Economics of Superabundant AI

NBER Working Paper

When compute exceeds available problems, prices collapse to zero. A formal economic model of the post-scarcity intuition.

Bridge

Studies in Mutualist Political Economy

Kevin Carson · Free online

The definitive synthesis of Austrian economics and Proudhonian mutualism. Uses Böhm-Bawerk's capital theory to build a left-libertarian economics. The most important bridge text.

Foundation

Governing the Commons

Elinor Ostrom · Cambridge University Press, 1990

Nobel Prize-winning proof that communities successfully self-govern shared resources without privatisation or state regulation. Eight design principles for commons that work.

Foundation

The Problem of Political Authority

Michael Huemer · Cambridge University Press, 2013

If an individual cannot legitimately coerce others, neither can a group — even by majority vote. The most rigorous modern argument that political authority is an illusion.

Foundation

Socialismo, cálculo económico y función empresarial

Jesús Huerta de Soto · Unión Editorial, 2010

The definitive Austrian School treatment of the socialist calculation debate. Extends Mises and Hayek with the theory of entrepreneurial function. Essential for understanding why central planning fails — and what might replace it.

Post-Scarcity 2025

Scarcity in an Age of AI Abundance

Papadogiannis · SSRN, 2025

How artificial intelligence reshapes the fundamental economic premise of scarcity.

Already Working at Scale

These are not thought experiments. Voluntary cooperation without the state is operational today, at massive scale, in the real world.

🐧

Linux

20,000+ developers. No price signals. No boss. Runs 96% of the world's top servers. The most successful commons in human history.

📚

Wikipedia

60 million articles, 300+ languages. Non-market allocation of editorial attention. Proof that large-scale coordination works without prices.

🌍

Fediverse

10M+ users across thousands of independent servers. Polycentric governance mapping Ostrom's eight principles. Structurally impossible to centralise.

☀️

Som Energia

80,000 members in Spain's largest energy cooperative. Renewable, democratic, no shareholders. Energy as a commons.

🏭

Mondragón

70,000 workers, €12 billion revenue. Cooperative since 1956. Survived the 2008 crisis better than capitalist competitors.

📡

guifi.net

40,000+ nodes. Spain's community mesh network. Telecoms without a corporation. Infrastructure as a commons.

🏗️

Rojava

Democratic confederalism at scale: millions of people in assemblies, cooperatives, self-managed education. Living proof that large-scale commons governance works now.

🗳️

Decidim

Barcelona's open-source participatory democracy platform. Used by 400+ organisations worldwide. Direct democracy as code.

🪙

Bitcoin & DAOs

Proof that decentralised coordination at global scale works without any central authority. $1T+ in value secured by math, not governments.

🌿

Kudumbashree

Kerala, India: 4.5 million women in self-governing community development networks. The largest women-led commons in the world.

🏠

Cooperation Jackson

Mississippi, USA: solidarity economy in one of America's poorest cities. Worker coops, community land trusts, and mutual aid — from below.

🔋

Brooklyn Microgrid

Peer-to-peer solar energy trading via blockchain. Neighbours selling surplus energy directly to each other. Energy commons in action.

Huemer proves political authority is illegitimate. AI proves it is obsolete. Together: the strongest case against the state ever constructed.
